01

Fixed Price

We agree the full scope up front, then deliver it for one agreed price. You know the total before we write any code — the certainty comes from pinning the scope down first.

Best when

Your scope is clear and stable and you need budget certainty — a well-defined MVP, or a specific feature with a hard deadline.

Risks Changes mid-way need a change request. It works badly if the requirements are still moving.
How cost works One fixed price for the agreed scope, usually paid against milestones.
How you stay in control Control lives in the spec — you approve the scope, we own delivering exactly that.
Real example A defined MVP with a fixed investor deadline and a set budget.
02

Time & Material

You pay for the time actually spent, billed monthly, and the scope flexes as you learn. You trade a fixed total for the freedom to change direction without renegotiating a contract every time.

Best when

The build will evolve, and you'd rather start fast and steer as you go than spec everything perfectly up front.

Risks The total is a range, not a fixed number — it needs trust and active prioritisation from you.
How cost works An hourly or daily rate on time spent; you see the burn transparently each month.
How you stay in control You re-prioritise the backlog every sprint and can change or stop at any time.
Real example A product still finding its shape, where requirements shift from sprint to sprint.
03

Dedicated Team

A full team on our payroll, working only on your product, for the long term — effectively your engineering team, run by us. You get the team without the hiring, management overhead, or payroll risk.

Best when

You have a long-term product with sustained work ahead and want a stable, dedicated squad rather than a one-off project.

Risks It's a monthly commitment — best value when there's genuinely enough work to keep the team busy.
How cost works A predictable monthly cost based on the team's size and composition.
How you stay in control You set the priorities and the roadmap; our delivery lead runs the day-to-day.
Real example A scaling product that needs a standing squad for 12 months and beyond.
04

Staff Augmentation / Outstaffing

Vetted engineers embedded into your existing team, under your management — you direct them day-to-day just like your own staff. It's added capacity or a specific skill, not a managed team.

Best when

You already have engineers and a manager, and you need to extend capacity or add a particular skill quickly.

Risks You provide the management, process, and direction — if you don't have those, a Dedicated Team fits better.
How cost works A simple monthly rate per engineer.
How you stay in control Full control — they work in your tools, your process, and to your direction.
Real example An in-house team that needs two React engineers for six months.
