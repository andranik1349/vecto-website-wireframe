How We Work

How we work — the part that matters more than the tech.

For a non-technical founder, the reassurance isn't the stack — it's knowing what working with us actually feels like. Here's our process, the ways you can engage us, and the reference detail underneath, each answering a question you're right to ask.

Get an estimate Schedule a call

The process

Five steps, from first conversation to live product.

The same shape every engagement: no surprises, no black box. This is the single most-asked-about thing — so it gets the most room.

1 Kickoff Align on goals, users, and what success looks like.
2 Scoping Turn the idea into a costed, sequenced plan.
3 Execute Design and build in tight iterations you can see.
4 Launch Ship to production with the safeguards in place.
5 Iterate Measure, improve, and keep the product evolving.

Engagement models

Four ways to work with us.

You've probably never hired an agency before — so we define every term in plain English and help you pick the one that fits where you are. The full page has a short decision tree if you're unsure.

Compare engagement models
Fixed Price Best when scope is clear and the budget is set. Time & Material Best when the build will evolve as you learn. Dedicated Team Best for a long-term product needing a standing team. Outstaffing Best when you have engineers and need to extend them.

The questions founders actually ask

Working with us, honestly.

Less than you fear, but not zero. Expect a weekly demo and a short check-in — roughly 2–3 hours a week — plus quick async answers when a decision needs you. We protect your time by bringing you the decisions that matter, not every detail.

One named delivery lead, for the life of the engagement. They're your single point of contact — no account-manager relay, no being passed between strangers. You'll also meet the people actually building your product at the demos.

We tell you what we'd do and why, with the trade-offs spelled out — then it's your call. Our job is to make sure you're deciding with the full picture, not to win the argument. Disagreement handled early is a lot cheaper than a surprise at launch.

Early and plainly. If something's slipping or a decision turned out wrong, you hear it at the next check-in — with options, not just a problem. The fastest way to lose your trust would be to let a small issue become a launch-day surprise, so we don't.
