Jira

Jira is where we track the work — so you can see exactly what's in progress, any time, without asking.

What it is

A shared board for the work.

Jira is a project-tracking tool. Every piece of work — a feature, a fix, a task — becomes a ticket on a shared board, organised into the current sprint. It's the single source of truth for what's planned, what's in progress, and what's done.

How we use it

Where Jira sits in an engagement.

Where it sits It's the backbone of the Scoping and Execute stages — the scoped backlog lives here, and each sprint's work is pulled from it onto the board.
What you see The board itself: tickets moving from To Do → In Progress → Done, the current sprint's goal, and what's coming next. The fortnightly demo walks through what moved.
Who updates it The delivery team keeps it current continuously — it's not a report we write for you, it's where the work actually happens.
Your access You're added from day one. Comment on a ticket, raise a new one, or just watch progress — no login request, no gatekeeping.

What it means for you

Why this matters to a founder.

Visibility

See exactly what's being worked on right now — no black box, no waiting for a status update to find out.

Collaboration

Weigh in where it counts — comment on a ticket, flag a priority, or add something you've thought of, in context.

Clean hand-off

A documented history of every decision and change — so whoever picks up the product next has the full story.
