Flexibility over dogma

How we work with your tools.

This is what we reach for by default — not a list of requirements. Where you already have a stack, we adapt to it.

We adapt to your stack

Already on Linear, GitLab, or your own board? We work there. We bring our defaults only when you don't have a preference.

You get real access

You're invited into the channels, boards, and repos — not kept at arm's length behind a status email.

We keep it lean

No five new tools you have to learn. We use the fewest surfaces that keep the work visible and moving.

Straight answers

About our tooling.

Almost always, yes. If your team already lives in Linear, GitLab, ClickUp, or your own setup, we work in it rather than forcing a migration. The only time we'll push back is if a tool genuinely can't support the way the work needs to run — and then we'll explain why and agree a path with you.

Yes — full visibility is the default, not an upgrade. You're added to the Slack channel, the board, and the repos from day one. You can see exactly what's in progress, what's next, and what's blocked, any time you want — no waiting for a weekly summary.

Deliberately. We use the fewest tools that do the job — typically one place to chat, one to track work, one for design, and the standard development kit. We'd rather you have two surfaces you actually check than seven you ignore, so we resist adding tools unless they earn their place.
