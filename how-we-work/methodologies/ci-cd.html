CI/CD

CI/CD is how we ship changes safely, many times a day — so improvements reach your users in hours, not in a nerve-wracking quarterly release.

What it is

Continuous Integration & Continuous Delivery.

In plain terms: instead of saving up months of changes and launching them all at once (and crossing our fingers), we ship small changes constantly, with automated checks catching mistakes before they ever reach you or your users.

More technically: Continuous Integration means every code change is automatically merged and tested against the whole system, many times a day. Continuous Delivery means any change that passes those checks can be released to production at the push of a button — or automatically. Together they replace risky big-bang releases with a steady, low-drama flow of small, verified updates.

Why we use it

Small and frequent beats big and rare.

A small change is easy to test, easy to review, and — if something does go wrong — easy to pinpoint and roll back. A giant quarterly release is the opposite: a tangle of changes where any one of them could be the culprit. CI/CD turns releases from a tense event into a non-event.

How dogmatic we are

We treat CI as essential on every project, however small — the automated test-and-merge safety net pays for itself immediately. Full automated deployment we tune to your context: a regulated product might keep a human approval step before production, and we'll design the pipeline around that rather than against it.

What it means for you

Why a non-technical founder should care.

Faster to your users

A fix or an improvement can be live the same day, not parked until the next big release.

Safer releases

Automated checks catch most problems before they ship, and a bad change can be rolled back in minutes.

Visible progress

Work flows to a staging environment continuously, so you can see real progress between demos.

How it works in practice

The cadence, artefacts, and tooling behind it.

Cadence Changes merge and deploy continuously — often many times a day. There's no "release week"; shipping is routine and ongoing.
Artefacts An automated test suite, a build pipeline that runs on every commit, a staging environment that mirrors production, and a deployment runbook.
Tooling The pipeline runs in the tools your team already knows. GitHub Actions Docker Jira
Safeguards Automated rollback, environment parity, and (where needed) a manual approval gate before production.
