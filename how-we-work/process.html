Our Process

From first conversation to live product — every step, no surprises.

This is how a single engagement runs, whichever service you're buying. Five steps, each with a clear duration, a defined output, and a named person on both sides. The #1 question we hear — "what's it actually like to work with you?" — answered up front.

01

Kickoff

Typically ~1 week

Before anyone designs or builds, we align on the goal. The whole point is to leave with a shared understanding of who this is for and what "done" actually means — so nothing downstream is built on a guess.

Deliverables Goals & success metrics, a user/persona snapshot, and a one-page project charter.
Who's involved You: founder + any key stakeholders. Us: delivery lead, product designer.
What you prepare Whatever you already have — a deck, sketches, a competitor list. No formal prep required.
Sample artefact A one-page project charter you sign off before scoping begins.
02

Scoping

Typically ~1–2 weeks

We turn the idea into a costed, sequenced plan you can actually decide on — what goes in the first release and what waits. You leave scoping knowing the price, the shape, and the trade-offs, not just a vague "it depends."

Deliverables A scoped backlog, an estimate (cost + timeline), a recommended engagement model, and an architecture sketch.
Who's involved You: founder. Us: delivery lead, designer, tech lead.
What you prepare Your priorities and any hard constraints — budget, launch date, compliance.
Sample artefact A costed scope document with an estimate range and a recommended model.
03

Execute

The bulk of the engagement

Design and build happen in two-week iterations. Every fortnight you see working software — not a status report — running on a staging environment you can click through yourself.

This is where founder anxiety is highest, so this is where visibility is highest too: a demo you attend, a decision log you can read, and a delivery lead who flags a slip the moment it appears, not at launch.

Deliverables A working increment each sprint, a demo every two weeks, a live staging environment, and a running decision log.
Who's involved You: at demos + async decisions. Us: the full squad — delivery lead, designer, engineers, QA.
What you prepare Roughly 2–3 hours a week for demos and the decisions only you can make.
Sample artefact A fortnightly demo recording + an updated, re-prioritised backlog.
04

Launch

Typically ~1 week

We ship to production with the safeguards in place — monitoring, alerting, and a tested rollback — and a go/no-go decision you make with the full picture in front of you. Launch day should be calm, because the risky parts were handled before it.

Deliverables Production deployment, monitoring & alerting, a runbook, and a signed-off launch checklist.
Who's involved You: the go/no-go call. Us: engineers, QA, DevOps.
What you prepare The launch date, and any marketing or comms you want timed to it.
Sample artefact A launch checklist + an operational runbook handed to you.
05

Iterate

Ongoing

Launch is the start, not the finish. We measure how the product is actually used, prioritise what to improve, and keep shipping on a steady cadence. If you want a long-term operating partner rather than a project team, this rolls into Support & Maintenance.

Deliverables A usage-analytics review, a prioritised improvement backlog, and a regular release cadence.
Who's involved You: founder. Us: delivery lead + a squad scaled to what you need next.
What you prepare Your goals for the next phase and any real-world feedback you're hearing.
Sample artefact A post-launch review + a next-quarter plan.

Made concrete

What your first 30 days look like.

The abstract becomes real fast. Here's a typical opening month from the day you say go.

Week 1

Kickoff & alignment

Goals workshop, charter signed, team introduced. You meet the people who'll build it.

Week 2

Scope & setup

Backlog scoped and estimated; repos, environments, and tooling stood up.

Week 3

First build increment

Design and the first working slice underway; staging environment live.

Week 4

First demo

You click through real, working software — and re-prioritise from what you see.

How we stay in sync

The rhythm you can count on.

Daily async updates

A short written update in a shared Slack channel — progress, blockers, anything that needs you. No daily meeting unless you want one.

Fortnightly demo

Every two weeks you see working software and re-prioritise. The single most important ritual — it's how you stay in control.

Monthly retro

A look back at what worked and what didn't — for both sides. How the partnership keeps improving, not just the product.

A clear escalation path

If something's urgent, your delivery lead responds within one business day — and you always know who to call.

Going deeper

Process questions, answered.

That's expected, not a problem — the fortnightly demo exists precisely so you can change course while it's cheap. We re-prioritise the backlog with you; on a Time & Material or Dedicated Team model that's frictionless, and even on Fixed Price we have a clear change process rather than a flat "no".

Small, visible increments make slippage obvious early — there's no 80%-done illusion when you see working software every two weeks. When something does slip, you hear it at the next check-in with options, not a surprise at the end.

Roughly 2–3 hours: the fortnightly demo, a short weekly check-in, and quick answers to the decisions only you can make. We deliberately bring you the high-leverage calls and handle the rest, so your time goes to the choices that actually shape the product.

You own everything — code, accounts, infrastructure, and a runbook — in your name. From there you can take it in-house, keep us on for Support & Maintenance, or pause and come back. There's no lock-in and no hostage-taking of your product.
