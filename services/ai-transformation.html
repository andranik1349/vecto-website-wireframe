Build AI into your product — with a clear idea of what you're actually getting.

The promise around AI is still louder than the reality. We've worked through that gap: integrating models, building pipelines, and learning where AI earns its place in a product and where it doesn't. That's the experience we bring to every AI engagement.

What's included

Three kinds of AI work — and they're not the same.

A non-technical founder can't be expected to tell these apart, so we keep them clearly separated: integrating existing models, building custom ones, and making sense of your data.

Integrate

Add AI to what you already have

Often the fastest, lowest-risk path: connect proven models to your product and shape their behaviour to your use case. No training runs, no research project.

How it works

Straight answers

For the sceptical founder.

Not necessarily. Off-the-shelf models often get you a long way before custom training becomes relevant — so a small or messy dataset isn't a blocker to starting.

The architecture isn't built on any single provider's stack by default. Models sit behind an abstraction so you can move between providers without rebuilding the product around them.

Success metrics are defined before the build, not after — accuracy, latency, and cost per inference among them — so "is it working?" has an answer you agreed to up front.

Components are modular, so models can be swapped as better ones arrive without rebuilding everything around them. That's a deliberate design choice, given how fast the field moves.

Failure modes are designed for, not hoped away: human review layers, fallback logic, and confidence thresholds so the system fails safely rather than confidently wrong.
