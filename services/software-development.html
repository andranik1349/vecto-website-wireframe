How a build runs

No black box. You'll know where we are at every step.

Select any step for what actually happens in it.

See the full process
1 Kickoff Align on goals, users, and what success looks like.

We sit down with you and the people who'll use this. By the end we share one definition of done — and you know who on our side owns what.

2 Scoping Turn the idea into a technical plan, estimate, and milestone map.

Architecture, stack, and a sprint-by-sprint plan with a real estimate behind it — so there are no surprises about cost or sequence once we start.

3 Execute Build in two-week sprints, with software you can use each one.

Every sprint ends with a working increment in your hands, a demo, and a chance to re-prioritise. This is where most of the time — and most of the reassurance — lives.

4 Launch Ship to production with monitoring, docs, and a clean handover.

Release isn't a cliff edge. We set up monitoring, write the docs your team will need, and walk you through running it before we step back.

5 Iterate Measure real usage and refine — the product keeps improving.

Once real users arrive, the data tells you what to do next. We keep iterating, or hand off to a maintenance plan — your call.

How we'd staff it

Four ways to work together. One that fits your build.

Fixed Price

Best when scope is clear.

A defined build for a firm number you can plan around — the usual fit for a well-specified MVP.

How it works

Time & Material

Best when the product will evolve.

Flexible scope billed for the work done, so you can steer priorities sprint to sprint as you learn.

How it works

Dedicated Team

Best when the build is long-running.

A stable, ring-fenced team that owns the product with you — on our payroll, focused on yours.

How it works

Outstaffing

Senior engineers plugged straight into your team and your process — managed by you, day to day.
