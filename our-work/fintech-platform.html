Case Study

Tessera: a payments platform two non-engineers got to market.

A founder pair with a finance background and no technical team came to us with an idea on a napkin: give small businesses one place to see and move their money. Eleven months later it was live, audited, and onboarding real customers.

FinTech SMB payments Software Development Product Design

Hero product visual — the live Tessera dashboard on desktop + mobile (device mockup or full-bleed key screen).

Where they started

Two founders, zero engineers, one spreadsheet.

Maya and Daniel had spent a decade inside SMB lending and knew the problem cold: the businesses they served juggled four banking portals, a spreadsheet, and a lot of guesswork. They had the domain expertise and the relationships — what they didn't have was a single line of code, an engineering hire, or a sense of what building this would cost.

They needed a partner who could carry the whole build, translate the regulatory reality into plain decisions, and not disappear once it shipped.

The challenge

"We know the problem. We have no idea how to build the thing."

The fear wasn't the product — it was everything around it. Could two finance people trust an agency with their entire idea? Would a payments platform that touches real money clear the compliance bar a bank's partnership team would scrutinise? And the quiet one underneath: would they spend their first cheque on something that never shipped?

Concretely, they needed to connect to business bank accounts securely, reconcile transactions across institutions, move money with an audit trail regulators would accept, and do it all in an interface a busy shop owner would actually use — without a CTO to tell them whether any of it was realistic.

Before: the spreadsheet-and-four-bank-portals workflow Tessera set out to replace.

Our approach

Frame the risk first, then build the smallest thing that proves it.

Before a line of code, we mapped the engagement around the two things that could kill it: regulatory exposure and burn rate. Every scope decision after that traced back to one of those.

Solution architecture diagram — bank aggregation, ledger/reconciliation service, payments rail, and the founder-facing app.

Buy the regulated parts, build the differentiated parts

We routed money movement and KYC through a licensed Banking-as-a-Service provider rather than chasing licences ourselves. Tessera's edge was the reconciliation and the experience — so that's where we spent the build.

Ruled out: becoming a regulated entity at MVP stage — months and legal cost the seed round couldn't absorb.

One killer workflow, not a banking suite

The MVP did exactly one thing brilliantly: see every account in one place and pay a bill from it. Cards, invoicing, and forecasting were named, scoped, and deliberately deferred to post-raise.

Ruled out: a broad v1 that would have doubled the timeline and diluted the demo.

Audit-readiness designed in, not bolted on

Immutable transaction logging, least-privilege access, and encryption were part of the first architecture decision — so the partner bank's due-diligence questionnaire was a confirmation, not a scramble.

Ruled in early: a SOC 2-aligned control set, because financial partners ask for it before they integrate.

Translate, don't just deliver

Every fortnight the founders got a demo and a plain-English decision log — no Jira archaeology required. They made the trade-off calls; we made sure they understood them.

Cross-link: this is our standard engagement process — Kickoff → Scoping → Execute → Launch → Iterate.

Outcomes

What changed for Tessera.

The MVP didn't just demo well — it cleared the partner bank's review, raised a round, and put real businesses on the platform inside a quarter.

11 mo From napkin to public launch
$2.4M Seed round raised on the live product
1,800 SMBs onboarded in the first 90 days
NPS 72 Among activated businesses
Outcome chart — onboarded businesses and payment volume across the first 90 days post-launch (before/after framing).

Before: a pitch deck and a prototype no investor could touch. After: a live, audited platform with paying users — the difference between "trust me" and "try it."

"We walked in with a spreadsheet and a hunch. VECTO turned it into something a bank's partnership team signed off on — and they explained every call along the way, so we always knew where our money was going."

Maya Okonkwo

Co-founder & CEO, Tessera

What's next

From MVP to the roadmap the raise unlocked.

The same dedicated team is still with Tessera, now building the features deliberately deferred at MVP: business cards, automated invoicing, and a cashflow forecast that turns their reconciliation data into a forward view. We're also supporting the move toward their own regulatory footprint as volume grows.

It's the relationship we aim for — not a handoff, but an operating partner that scales with the company.
